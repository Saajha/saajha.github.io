Operational Model

The operational model can be classified in two broad categories-

Saajha Impact Schools

Saajha's inside school interventions, outside school interventions and biannual workshops have resulted in an improvement in both, leadership capabilities of the School Management Committees and improvement in learning outcomes.

From left to Right – an inside school intervention, community engagement activity and a biannual workshop

Interventions in Saajha Impact schools are directly led by the team members of the Saajha. There are three kinds of interventions undertaken in these schools –
  1. Inside school interventions – These interventions focus on facilitation of the SMC meetings and activities for building leadership capabilities of the school staff.
  2. Community engagement activities – These activities focus on raising awareness about parental and community engagement in children learning.
  3. Biannual workshops – Saajha conducts biannual workshops which form a platform for the SMCs across the zones to interact with each other and share their learning experiences.

Saajha's interventions are led by a team of facilitators, with each facilitator undertaking 12 school visits across 8 schools and one community visit every week. A team of 4-5 facilitators is led by a Cohort leader who is tasked with providing strategic guidance to the interventions in schools. Saajha currently operates in 87 schools through Saajha Impact Schools model.

Saajhedar Schools

Saajha is actively looking at partnerships to implement the SMC leadership-building module across the country. In Saajhedar schools, Saajha works with a partner to build effective practices of SMC strengthening. A major initiative with regard to Saajhedar schools has been Saajha's partnership with Delhi government to build capacity of SMCs across the state. Delhi currently has 1207 schools. To transform the status quo of SMCs across the state, Saajha has devised a three-pronged approach which would focus on building capabilities of the trainers and volunteers who could in turn build capabilities of the School Management committees.
